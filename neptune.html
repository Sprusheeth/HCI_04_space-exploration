IN DEPTH

Neptune's environment is probably not conducive to life as we know it. The temperatures, pressures and materials that characterize this planet are most likely too extreme and volatile for organisms to adapt to.

While planet Neptune is an unlikely place for living things to take hold, the same is not true of some of its many moons. Triton is one of the likeliest places to find life elsewhere in our solar system. There is evidence of a subsurface ocean just beneath its icy crust, where life could possibly be supported.

The temperature in the clouds of Neptune is about minus 353 degrees Fahrenheit. The core temperature may be about 5,000 degrees Fahrenheit. That's hotter than the surface of the sun!

Neptune has 14 confirmed moons, with 5 more recently discovered that are yet to be officially named. Among these, the four largest and most significant are Triton, Nereid, Proteus, and Larissa.

Neptune boasts over 14 moons, each with unique characteristics that add to the complexity of its system. Triton, the largest, has a thick atmosphere and lakes of methane, while Nereid, famous for its icy geysers, hints at a subsurface ocean.
