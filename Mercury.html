MOONS

Mercury: The Moonless Planet Mercury, the smallest planet in our solar system, stands out for its lack of natural satellites or moons. Unlike other planets such as Earth, which has one moon, or Jupiter, which boasts a staggering 79, Mercury orbits the Sun alone. This absence of moons is likely due to its proximity to the Sun, where the gravitational forces are too strong to allow for the stable formation or capture of a satellite. This unique characteristic makes Mercury an intriguing subject of study in planetary science.
