FEATURES

Mars has several distinct features, including the largest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons, and the longest valley in the Solar System, Valles Marineris.

OLYMPUS MONS

Olympus Mons is the largest volcano in the Solar System, with a height of over 27 km and a base diameter of over 600 km. It is located in the Tharsis region of Mars and is considered one of the most impressive geological features in the Solar System.

  • Olympus Mons is around three times the height of Mount Everest.
  • It is the largest known volcano in the Solar System.
  • The volcano is around 600 km wide and has a total volume of around 2.5 million cubic kilometers.

MOONS

Mars has two small natural satellites, Phobos and Deimos. Both moons were discovered in 1877 by astronomer Asaph Hall.

Phobos

Phobos is the larger of Mars's two moons, measuring about 22 km in diameter. It has an irregular shape, resembling an asteroid.

Phobos orbits Mars at a very close distance of approximately 6,000 km, closer to its planet than any other moon in the Solar System. Because of its proximity, Phobos completes an orbit in just over 7 hours, meaning it rises and sets twice each Martian day.

Scientists believe Phobos may be a captured asteroid from the nearby asteroid belt, or possibly a remnant from Mars's formation. Its surface is covered with deep grooves and craters, the largest being Stickney Crater.

Due to gravitational interactions, Phobos is slowly spiraling towards Mars and is expected to either crash into the planet or break up into a ring in about 50 million years.
